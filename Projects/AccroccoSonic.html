Accrocco Description

The Accrocco is a unique wooden sculpture created in 2015, designed as an experimental musical instrument. Its structure and functionality combine acoustic principles with artistic creativity, resulting in a versatile and intriguing sound source.

Structure

  • Base Materials: The sculpture is primarily constructed from wood and plastic.
  • Core Design: Two large wooden panels are held together by two rectangular wooden poles, forming a stable framework.
  • Bridge: A flexible plastic strip is mounted between the two poles in a V-shaped configuration. This plastic strip serves as the bridge where the strings are attached on one side.

Strings and Vibrations

  • The Accrocco features three strings stretched across the structure.
  • A pickup is mounted to capture the vibrations of the strings, allowing their sound to be amplified or processed.
  • The flexibility of the plastic strip creates a dynamic interaction with the strings. When plucked, the strings' vibrations cause the bridge to bend, producing a momentary fluctuation in their frequency. This adds a unique modulation effect to the sound, making the instrument capable of generating complex and evolving tones.

Sound Characteristics

The combination of string vibrations and the bending bridge introduces rich, unpredictable harmonic textures. The resulting sounds are both organic and mechanically intriguing, offering a wide spectrum of sonic possibilities.
