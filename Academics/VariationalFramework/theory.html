Variational Geometric Network Framework

The SimplesModel is founded on the radical premise that geometry is not merely a passive container for physical processes, but an active agent of action and materialization. Within this framework, we move away from traditional simulations of matter and toward a pure, variational evolution of form.

The Ontology of the Simple

A Simple is defined as a closed, deformable surface whose existence is entirely characterized by its intrinsic geometry. Existing without a surrounding physical medium, it possesses a form of "geometric agency" driven by the minimization of its internal energy. Every movement or deformation is a variational response to its own curvature and area—an inherent drive toward equilibrium.

Core Governing Principles

The behavior of these agents is governed by a set of interlocking principles that ensure both local integrity and global connectivity:

Intrinsic Drive and Morphogenesis: Each Simple is motivated by a surface energy functional that penalizes deviations from a reference state. Primarily, this manifests as a tendency toward spherical configurations, where surface tension and bending rigidity reach a geometric truce.

The Constraint of Area Invariance: Unlike a physical bubble that might expand or contract based on pressure, a Simple adheres to strict Per-Simple Area Conservation. This constraint is the engine of the model’s complexity: because the total area is fixed, a local deformation in one region must be instantaneously compensated by an inverse adjustment elsewhere.

Connectivity and Shared Materiality: When Simples meet, they do not merely collide; they form shared surface patches or films. These interactions introduce extrinsic forces that couple the agents into a network. In this state, the "will" of one Simple becomes inextricably linked to the geometry of its neighbors, creating a unified, responsive system.

Variational Evolution over Linear Time: The model replaces the standard notion of physical time with a logical sequence of energy-decreasing states. Evolution is not a march along a clock, but a branching path through an Energy Landscape. Each transition selects a new state that lowers the total energy, fundamentally altering the map of all future possibilities.

A Bridge to Compositional Logic

This framework serves as a conceptual bridge between discrete geometric networks and emergent structures in music and art. By treating geometry as an agent, we can observe non-local branching and history-dependent growth—phenomena where a single geometric "nudge" triggers a cascade of structural reconfigurations. The result is a system that does not simply exist in space, but actively participates in its own becoming.
