Variational Geometric Network Framework

The SimplesModel operates on a fundamental paradigm shift: treating geometry not as a static container, but as an active agent of materialization.

A Simple is defined as a closed, deformable geometric entity (a 2-manifold) existing in a medium-less vacuum. Its "will" is expressed through the minimization of an energy functional—balancing surface tension against bending rigidity—while adhering to a strict topological constraint: Per-Simple Area Conservation.

Core Governing Principles

Principle Geometric Mechanism Systemic Result
Intrinsic Drive Mean curvature minimization ($H \to H_0$) Each Simple inherently seeks a balanced, spherical equilibrium.
Area Invariance $\int_{\Sigma} dA = A_0$ Local impacts force instantaneous, nonlocal redistributions across the surface.
Shared Materiality Shared film/patch formation Interaction energy ($E_{int}$) couples neighboring agents into a unified network.
Variational Time State-ordering via $\delta E < 0$ The system evolves through "logical steps" rather than linear clock-time.

From Geometry to Composition

This framework serves as a conceptual bridge between discrete geometric networks and emergent compositional structures. In the context of music and art:

  • Non-Local Branching: A single geometric "nudge" in one corner of the network can trigger a cascade of structural reconfigurations elsewhere, mimicking the organic development of a musical theme.
  • History Dependence: Because every transition alters the energy landscape, the system possesses a "geometric memory"—the path taken fundamentally changes the set of future possibilities.
"The Object is generative, nonlinear, and history-dependent: it does not just move; it becomes."
